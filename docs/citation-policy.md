# Citation Policy

University AI Policy Tracker publishes tracker metadata and source-backed claims for citation. It does not republish official university documents as open content.

Canonical public domain: `https://eduaipolicy.org`. Citations should use canonical pages and public JSON records under this domain once production is live.

## Public Claim Requirements

Every public claim must include:

- canonical entity slug and canonical URL
- claim text and claim type
- confidence
- review state
- source URL
- source snapshot hash
- short evidence snippet
- source attribution and rights caveat

Claims without evidence stay internal or remain `needs_review`.

## Confidence And Review State

`confidence` is the machine confidence in extraction or classification. It is not a review decision.

`reviewState` records workflow state:

- `machine_candidate`: generated by crawler, extraction, or local seed data.
- `agent_reviewed`: checked by a reviewing agent but not necessarily human approved.
- `human_reviewed`: approved by a human reviewer or deterministic publish rule.
- `needs_review`: ambiguous, weakly supported, stale, or blocked.
- `rejected`: not suitable for public use.

Public pages should make this distinction visible.

## Rights

Tracker metadata may be published under `CC-BY-4.0`.

Official source documents, source page text, PDFs, and other university materials retain their original rights and terms. Evidence snippets must stay short, source-attributed, and necessary for verification.

## Suggested Citation

Use the canonical page and JSON record together:

```text
{University name} AI Policy Tracker record. University AI Policy Tracker. Last checked {YYYY-MM-DD}. {canonical URL}
```

Example production canonical URL shape:

```text
https://eduaipolicy.org/universities/{slug}
```

When citing a specific claim, also retain the source URL, snapshot hash, and evidence snippet from the public JSON.
